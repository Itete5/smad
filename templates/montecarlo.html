Monte Carlo

Configure and run Monte Carlo simulations: MCMC, kinetic Monte Carlo (kMC), Parallel Tempering, and Grand Canonical Monte Carlo (GCMC) for sampling, kinetics, and phase behavior.

Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)

Sample from posterior distributions (e.g. parameter fitting, uncertainty quantification). Configure proposal distribution, chain length, burn-in, and convergence diagnostics.

  • Metropolis–Hastings, Gibbs sampling, or Hamiltonian MC
  • Link to likelihood from DFT or experiments
  • Output: chains, autocorrelation, credible intervals

Kinetic Monte Carlo (kMC)

Simulate rare events and long-time dynamics (e.g. diffusion, surface kinetics, defect evolution). Define events, rates, and lattice or off-lattice model.

  • Event lists and rate constants (from barriers or models)
  • Residence-time algorithm or BKL
  • Output: trajectories, mean first-passage times, statistics

Parallel Tempering (replica exchange)

Run replicas at different temperatures and swap configurations to improve sampling and escape local minima. Useful for phase transitions and rugged landscapes.

  • Temperature schedule and swap scheme
  • Compatible with MCMC or molecular dynamics
  • Output: acceptance ratios, free-energy estimates

Grand Canonical Monte Carlo (GCMC)

Sample at fixed chemical potential (and T, V) for adsorption, insertion, and open-system equilibria. Insertion/deletion and displacement moves.

  • Chemical potential, box size, interaction model
  • Widom or histogram methods for μ
  • Output: adsorption isotherms, density, fluctuations
